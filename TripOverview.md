## Weather Preview

**General pattern:** Late March in Japan follows "San-kan-shi-on" — three cold days, four warm days. Expect cool mornings, mild afternoons, and chilly evenings. Rain is likely on 2-3 days.

| Location | Dates | Highs | Lows | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|------|-------|
| **Tokyo** | Mar 20-24 | 13-16°C (55-61°F) | 5-8°C (41-46°F) | Comfortable with layers. |
| **Fujikawaguchiko** | Mar 24-25 | ~9°C (48°F) | ~0°C (32°F) | **The cold one.** Near-freezing at night. Real coat needed. |
| **Kyoto** | Mar 25-27 | 13-15°C (55-59°F) | 4-7°C (39-45°F) | Similar to Tokyo. Early mornings at Fushimi Inari will be cold. |
| **Hiroshima** | Mar 27-28 | 14-16°C (57-61°F) | 6-8°C (43-46°F) | Slightly warmer. |
| **Osaka** | Mar 28-29 | 14-16°C (57-61°F) | 6-8°C (43-46°F) | Slightly warmer. |

**Cherry blossom status:** You'll hit the start of the season. Shinjuku Gyoen (Tokyo) and Maruyama Park (Kyoto) have early-blooming weeping cherry trees (Kawazu-zakura) that should be pink during your dates.

## Japan Etiquette Quick Guide

### The Big Rules

- **No tipping.** Ever. Not at restaurants, hotels, taxis, or bars. It can be seen as rude.
- **No eating or drinking while walking.** Finish your food at the stall or stand to the side. Trains are also a no-go (Shinkansen is the exception).
- **No talking on the phone on trains.** Set it to silent (manner mode). Texting is fine.
- **No loud conversations in public transit.** Keep voices low, especially on subways.
- **Carry your trash.** Public trash cans barely exist. Keep a plastic bag on you.

### Shoes

- **Remove shoes when entering** homes, ryokans, some restaurants, temples, and fitting rooms. Look for a raised entryway (genkan) or a shoe shelf — that's your cue.
- **Wear clean, presentable socks.** You will be taking your shoes on and off constantly.
- **Toilet slippers.** Some places have separate slippers for the bathroom. Use them, and don't forget to switch back.

### Temples & Shrines

- **Bow slightly** before entering through a torii gate. Walk to the side, not the center (the center path is for the gods).
- **Purification fountain (temizuya):** Rinse left hand, then right, then pour water into your left hand and rinse your mouth. Don't drink directly from the ladle.
- **Photos:** Usually fine outside. Check for signs inside — some halls prohibit it.
- **Hats off** inside temple buildings.

### Restaurants & Dining

- **Say "Itadakimasu"** (いただきます) before eating — it means "I humbly receive." Hands together, small bow.
- **Say "Gochisousama deshita"** (ごちそうさまでした) when leaving — it means "thank you for the meal."
- **Chopstick rules:**
  - Never stick them upright in rice (funeral ritual).
  - Never pass food chopstick-to-chopstick (also funeral ritual).
  - Rest them on the chopstick holder, not across your bowl.
- **Slurping noodles is encouraged.** It means you're enjoying it.
- **Pour drinks for others** before yourself. Someone will pour yours.
- **Hassun-specific:** No cologne or perfume. Kaiseki is about the aroma of the food.

### Onsen (Hot Springs — Fuji)

- **You go in naked.** No swimsuits.
- **Wash thoroughly** at the shower stations before entering the bath. Soap, shampoo, full rinse.
- **Small towel:** Bring it with you but don't let it touch the water. Fold it on your head.
- **Tattoos:** Some onsen prohibit them. Check in advance or look for "tattoo-friendly" spots.
- **No phones.** Leave them in the locker.

### Convenience Stores & Shopping

- **Use both hands** when giving or receiving money, cards, or items. It shows respect.
- **Don't open food inside the store.** Pay first, eat outside or at the in-store counter.
- **Conbini (7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart)** ATMs accept foreign cards — this is the easiest way to get cash.

### General

- **Bowing:** A slight nod/bow covers most situations. You don't need to go deep — just acknowledge it.
- **Queuing:** Lines are sacred. Never cut. Platforms have marked queuing spots for trains.
- **Escalators:** Stand on the left in Tokyo, left in most cities (Osaka is the exception — stand on the right).
- **Pointing:** Use your whole hand (palm up) instead of a single finger when gesturing at something.
