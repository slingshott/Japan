## Weather Preview

**General pattern:** Late March in Japan follows "San-kan-shi-on" — three cold days, four warm days. Expect cool mornings, mild afternoons, chilly evenings. Rain likely on 2–3 days.

| Location | Dates | Highs | Lows | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|------|-------|
| **Tokyo** | Mar 20-24 | 13-16°C (55-61°F) | 5-8°C (41-46°F) | Comfortable with layers. |
| **Fujikawaguchiko** | Mar 24-25 | ~9°C (48°F) | ~0°C (32°F) | The cold one. Near-freezing at night. |
| **Kyoto** | Mar 25-27 | 13-15°C (55-59°F) | 4-7°C (39-45°F) | Early mornings at Fushimi Inari will be cold. |
| **Hiroshima** | Mar 27-28 | 14-16°C (57-61°F) | 6-8°C (43-46°F) | Slightly warmer. |
| **Osaka** | Mar 28-29 | 14-16°C (57-61°F) | 6-8°C (43-46°F) | Slightly warmer. |

**Cherry blossom:** Start of the season. Shinjuku Gyoen (Tokyo) and Maruyama Park (Kyoto) have early-blooming weeping cherry trees.

## Japan Etiquette Quick Guide

### The Big Rules

- No tipping. Ever.
- No eating or drinking while walking. Finish at the stall or stand aside.
- No phone calls on trains. Silent (manner) mode. Texting is fine.
- Keep voices low on public transit.
- Carry your trash. Public bins barely exist.

### Shoes

- Remove shoes when entering homes, ryokans, some restaurants, temples, fitting rooms.
- Wear clean, presentable socks. Toilet slippers: use them and switch back.

### Temples & Shrines

- Bow slightly before a torii gate. Walk to the side, not the center.
- Purification fountain: rinse left hand, right hand, then mouth (from left hand). Don’t drink from the ladle.
- Hats off inside temple buildings.

### Restaurants & Dining

- "Itadakimasu" (いただきます) before eating. "Gochisousama deshita" (ごちそうさまでした) when leaving.
- Never stick chopsticks upright in rice. Never pass food chopstick-to-chopstick.
- Slurping noodles is encouraged. Pour drinks for others before yourself.
- Hassun: No cologne or perfume.

### Onsen (Fuji)

- You go in naked. Wash thoroughly at shower stations before entering the bath.
- Small towel: don’t let it touch the water. Tattoos: check policy in advance.

### General

- Use both hands when giving or receiving money or items.
- Queuing: never cut. Escalators: stand left in Tokyo, right in Osaka.
- 7-Eleven / Lawson / FamilyMart ATMs accept foreign cards.
