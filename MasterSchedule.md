## Potential Issues & Major Misses

**The "Luggage at Himeji" Disaster:** I cannot stress this enough. On Friday, March 27th, your itinerary has you stopping at Himeji Castle on the way to Hiroshima. You will have the entire group's luggage. The coin lockers at Himeji Station will almost certainly be full by the time you arrive at 8:49 AM during Cherry Blossom season. You must ship your large bags from Kyoto to Hiroshima the day before.

**The "Suntory Distillery" Trap:** Your original Wanderlog had the Suntory Yamazaki Distillery listed on the travel day from Fuji to Kyoto (March 25). Trying to stop here with 7 people and luggage is a logistical nightmare. Unless you have confirmed tour reservations (which book up months in advance), skip it and go straight to Kyoto.

**Cherry Blossom Timing:** You will be in Japan for the start of the Cherry Blossom season, but likely miss the "Full Bloom" (Mankai) in the major cities. To ensure you see good pink blooms, explicitly add Shinjuku Gyoen (Tokyo) and Maruyama Park (Kyoto) to your daytime walking routes, as they feature early-blooming weeping cherry trees.

**Reserving the "Oversized Baggage" Seats:** For your Shinkansen rides (Tokyo -> Fuji -> Kyoto -> Hiroshima -> Osaka), if you are bringing large suitcases, you must reserve the "Oversized Baggage" seats at the back of the train cars. If you just buy standard tickets, there is nowhere to put large luggage. If you utilize luggage forwarding between cities, this becomes a non-issue.
