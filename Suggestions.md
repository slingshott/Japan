## 1. Cherry Blossom Timing (When & Where)

You'll be in Japan right at the start of cherry blossom season. You'll likely miss peak bloom in most cities, but early-blooming varieties (weeping cherries, Kawazu-zakura) will be pink during your dates. Here's where to slot them in:

### Shinjuku Gyoen (Tokyo)
- **Best fit:** Saturday March 21 morning (7:00 AM – 12:00 PM), while you and Megan are exploring before the floral workshop. OR Tuesday March 24 morning before the 1:30 PM train to Fuji.
- **Why:** 65 varieties of cherry trees including early-blooming types. ¥500 entry. No alcohol allowed (it's a park, not a party). Gorgeous for photos.
- **Note:** The Wanderlog already flags this as a must-see for early Kawazu-zakura.

### Maruyama Park (Kyoto)
- **Best fit:** Wednesday March 25 evening, right when you arrive in Kyoto and explore Gion. The famous weeping cherry tree is illuminated at night.
- **Why:** Kyoto's most famous cherry blossom spot. The giant weeping cherry (shidarezakura) is lit up after dark, and the park is right in Gion — a natural add to your Wednesday evening walk.
- **Bonus:** If you go at night, it doubles as a hanami atmosphere with food stalls and lanterns.

## 3. Miyajima Island (Hiroshima — Friday March 27)

This is the biggest thing I think you're missing. Miyajima is a 25-minute ferry ride from Hiroshima and home to the iconic floating torii gate (Itsukushima Shrine) — one of Japan's three most celebrated views.

### The Problem
Your Friday schedule is already packed: Himeji Castle (morning), train to Hiroshima (afternoon), Peace Memorial Museum, Okonomimura (dinner). There's no room.

### The Fix
**Move Okonomimura to a late lunch (~3:30-4:30 PM)** after the Peace Memorial Museum, then take the ferry to Miyajima for sunset and the illuminated torii gate at night. OR skip the Peace Memorial Museum (controversial, but it is emotionally heavy and takes 2+ hours) and do Miyajima afternoon + Okonomimura for a late dinner.

### Alternative
If Friday is too tight, you could go Saturday morning before the 9:07 AM train to Osaka — but that's extremely early (first ferry is ~6:30 AM). Probably not realistic.

**My honest take:** If you can only pick two of {Peace Memorial Museum, Miyajima, Okonomimura}, I'd pick Miyajima + Okonomimura. But all three might be doable if you hustle.

## 4. Kyoto — Wednesday Evening Additions (March 25)

You arrive in Kyoto at 2:34 PM and the evening is listed as "Explore Gion district." Here are ways to make that evening special:

### Sannenzaka & Ninenzaka (Higashiyama)
- Beautiful preserved streets with traditional wooden shops leading up toward Kiyomizu-dera.
- Gorgeous at dusk. Less crowded in the evening than during the day.
- 15-minute walk from Gion.

### Kiyomizu-dera at Dusk
- The temple itself closes at 6 PM, but the approach streets and the views from outside are stunning at golden hour.
- If there's a special nighttime illumination happening (common in late March), the temple reopens at 6:30 PM for evening viewing.

### Maruyama Park Illuminated Cherry Tree (see Section 1)
- Combine with Gion exploration. Walk Gion → Maruyama Park → Sannenzaka → back to the hotel.

### Pontocho Alley for Dinner
- Narrow lantern-lit alley along the Kamogawa River, packed with small restaurants.
- Great for a group dinner on the first Kyoto night. No reservation needed for many spots — just walk in.

## 5. Kyoto — Thursday Morning (March 26) — The Fushimi Inari Enhancement

You're going to Fushimi Inari at 6-7 AM which is smart. A few additions:

### Go All the Way to the Top
- Most tourists stop at the first few torii gates. The full hike to the summit takes ~1.5-2 hours round trip and the higher you go, the emptier it gets.
- The summit has a small shrine and views of Kyoto.

### Breakfast at Vermillion Cafe
- Right at the base of Fushimi Inari. Excellent coffee and egg sandwiches.
- Perfect refuel after the hike before heading to Nishiki Market.

## 6. Osaka — Saturday Evening (March 28)

Your evening in Osaka is just "Dotonbori." That's a good start but Osaka's food scene deserves more structure:

### Shinsekai District (Before or After Dotonbori)
- Retro neighborhood south of Namba with the Tsutenkaku Tower, Jan-Jan Yokocho alley, and the best kushikatsu (deep-fried skewers) in Osaka.
- Grittier, more "local" vibe than Dotonbori. Great contrast.
- **The move:** Kushikatsu at Daruma (the famous chain) or any stall on the alley. Rule: never double-dip in the communal sauce.

### Dotonbori Must-Hits
- **Takoyaki:** Wanaka or Kukuru (both near the Glico sign).
- **Gyoza:** Osaka Ohsho.
- **Cheesecake:** Rikuro Ojisan (famous jiggly cheesecake, ¥765). The line moves fast.

### Hozenji Yokocho
- Tiny, atmospheric alley right off Dotonbori with a moss-covered Buddhist statue (Fudo Myo-o). Splash water on it for good luck.
- The alley has intimate bars and restaurants. Good for a quiet drink after the Dotonbori chaos.

## 8. Fuji — Tuesday Evening (March 24)

The schedule says "Relax, enjoy Mt. Fuji views, Onsen." A few specific ideas:

### Sunset Viewing from Oishi Park
- 15-minute drive from your rental. Famous lavender fields and an unobstructed Fuji view across Lake Kawaguchi.
- Sunset in late March is around 5:50 PM. Get there by 5:15 PM.

### Onsen Recommendation: Yurari
- "Fuji Yurari" has indoor and outdoor baths with a direct Fuji view.
- ~¥1,300 entry. Towel rental available. Open until 9 PM.
- Tattoo policy varies — worth calling ahead or checking online.

### Houtou (Local Dish)
- Fuji area's signature dish: thick flat noodles in a rich miso broth with vegetables.
- **Houtou Fudou** is the most famous restaurant (expect a wait). There's a branch near the lake.

## 10. Practical Suggestions

### Conbini Breakfast Strategy
Don't over-plan breakfast. Japanese convenience stores (7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart) have incredible cheap food: onigiri (¥120-180), egg sandwiches, nikuman (meat buns), and good coffee. This saves time and money on busy mornings.

### IC Card Loading
Load your Suica/Pasmo with at least ¥3,000-5,000 on arrival. You'll use it for trains, convenience stores, vending machines, and some restaurants. Reloading at machines takes 30 seconds.

### Coin Lockers at Stations
For the Fuji-to-Kyoto travel day (March 25), if anyone wants to break off from the group at Kyoto Station, the coin lockers at Kyoto Station fill up fast. Use the ones in the underground mall (Porta) — they're larger and less known.

### Google Maps Transit Directions
Google Maps is extremely accurate for Japanese transit. It gives you the exact platform number, car number, and transfer walking time. Trust it completely.

### Cash Withdrawal
Withdraw yen at 7-Eleven ATMs (they accept all foreign cards). Plan for roughly ¥5,000-10,000/day per person for food, transit top-ups, and small purchases. Bigger restaurants and hotels take cards.

### Pharmacies
If you need anything medical: look for "ドラッグストア" (drug store). Matsumoto Kiyoshi and Welcia are everywhere. Staff often speak limited English, but Google Translate camera mode works on packaging.
